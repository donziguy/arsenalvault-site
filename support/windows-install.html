Installing Arsenal Vault on Windows 11

A step-by-step guide to downloading and installing the Arsenal Vault Desktop application on your Windows 11 computer.

Before You Begin

Windows may display a security prompt when installing Arsenal Vault. This is normal for new applications. The steps below will guide you through the installation process safely.

1 Download the Installer

Visit the Arsenal Vault download page and click the Windows Desktop download button. Your browser will download a file named ArsenalVault-Setup.exe.

The file will typically appear in your Downloads folder or at the bottom of your browser window.

2 Run the Installer

Double-click the downloaded file to start the installation. Windows will display a security screen — this is expected.

You'll see a screen that says "Windows protected your PC"

Windows SmartScreen prompt

This message appears because Arsenal Vault is a new application. It's safe to proceed — your data stays encrypted on your local machine.

3 Click "More info"

On the Windows security screen, click the "More info" link. This reveals additional options.

Click "More info"

This text link appears below the warning message

Click "More info" to reveal the Run button

4 Click "Run anyway"

After clicking "More info," a "Run anyway" button will appear. Click it to proceed with the installation.

Click "Run anyway" to start installation

The Arsenal Vault installer will now launch.

5 Complete the Installation

Follow the installer prompts to complete the setup:

  • Accept the license agreement
  • Choose your installation location (default is recommended)
  • Click "Install" and wait for the process to complete
  • Click "Finish" to launch Arsenal Vault

Installation Complete!

Arsenal Vault is now installed. You can find it in your Start menu or on your desktop. Your data is stored locally and encrypted with AES-256 encryption.

Troubleshooting

The "More info" link doesn't appear

If you only see a "Don't run" button, make sure you're clicking directly on the blue "More info" text, not elsewhere on the dialog. The text may be small — look for it below the main warning message.

Windows blocks the download entirely

Some browsers may block the download. Try right-clicking the download link and selecting "Save link as..." or temporarily disable your browser's download protection in settings.

Antivirus software flags the installer

Some antivirus programs may flag new applications. You can temporarily disable your antivirus during installation, or add an exception for Arsenal Vault. The application is safe and does not contain malware.

Your Data Stays Secure

Arsenal Vault Desktop stores all your data locally on your computer — it never leaves your machine. Your vault is encrypted with AES-256, the same standard used by banks and government agencies. Even if someone accessed your computer, they couldn't read your inventory without your password.
